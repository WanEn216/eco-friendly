Switch to Reusable Items

There are plenty of everyday items. Some you might not have even considered.

1. Reusable Cleaning Clothes

Swap: Paper towels For: Reusable cleaning cloths or sponges

Every time you use a paper towel, it's one more piece of waste added to the landfill. Instead, opt for reusable cleaning clothes or sponges. These are machine washable, long-lasting, adn effective for cleaning messes around the house

Tip: Keep a set of reusable cleaning cloths in your kitchen, and use them for cleaning spills, wiping down counters and drying dishes.

2. Reusable Food Storage Bags and Wraps

Swap: Plastic sandwich bags and cling film For: Beeswax wraps or silicone food storage bads

Plastic sandwich bags and cling film are often used for packing snacks and leftovers, but there are much more sustainable options. Beeswax wraps are perfect for wrapping up sandwiches, fruits, and other snacks. They are washable and reusable, making them an eco-friendly alternative to plastic wrap. Silicone food storage bags are another great option for storing food, as they are durable, reusable, and safe to use in the freezer.

Tips: Use beeswax wraps to cover your lunch box of tiffin to keep food fresh without resorting to single-use plastic bags.

How to Get Started with Reusable Swaps

1. Take it Slow: Making the switch doesn't need to happen all at once. Start by swapping out one or two disposable items for reusable alternatives and gradually work your way up. 2. Invest in Quality: Reusable items are often more durable than their disposable counterparts, so it's worth investing in high-quality products that will last. 3. Be Prepared: Keep reusable items like your lunch box,water bottle, and shopping bags with you so you're always ready to make sustainable choices when you're out and about.
